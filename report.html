Purpose

Purpose of the website

This website offers Financial Well-Being Coaching for women. Minna, the financial coach, assists women in creating their "balance sheet", enabling a comprehensive understanding of their entire financial situation. This includes guidance on effectively managing monthly and yearly financial flows. Surprisingly, numerous women, even those in high-paying roles, have not embarked on their investment journeys and often find parts of their finances in disarray. This website primarily promotes a 6-week coaching program while providing useful information such as resources for self-study and a newsletter archive to rectify these issues and empower women towards financial independence.

Structure of the website

  • First Page (Home)

    The initial page will greet visitors with a compelling message about the importance of investing, emphasizing the essence of financial well-being. This narrative also shares the financial coach's personal journey, particularly her realization of the importance of investment during her quest with her husband to buy their dream abode. Furthermore, several testimonials from satisfied clients will be showcased in a carousel format to keep the page concise. Action buttons, such as "Subscribe to Newsletter" and "Book the First Free Session", will be positioned strategically at the page's conclusion, ensuring it doesn't come off as overly aggressive.

  • Second Page (Course)

    This section delves deeper into the courses tailored for enhancing women's financial management skills. It spotlights a 6-week course split into five detailed sessions, explaining the learning outcomes and setting expectations. A clear visual representation will highlight the five course modules using a grid layout. An accordion design is incorporated for in-depth information to ensure the amount of information doesn't appear overwhelming, while providing detailed information for those interested.

  • Third Page (Resource)

    Catering to those who might prefer not to take the courses at least for now, this page offers an array of resources, from books to podcasts, bolstering independent financial management knowledge. Each resource is neatly encapsulated within a Bootstrap card design, with convenient links directing to platforms like Apple Podcasts, Spotify for podcasts, and Amazon for books.

  • Fourth Page (Newsletter)

    This segment is exclusively dedicated to the newsletter. The financial coach offers daily insights, nudging women to be continually aware of their financial well-being. Recognizing a preference for audio/visual contents over text, several posts offer video messages supplemented by detailed articles. For enhanced user experience, popular posts are equipped with video playback options. Furthermore, posts are chronologically arranged, with the freshest content up top, complemented by a topic-based filter system like "investing," "well-being," "mission," and "collaboration." Upon clicking the "read more" button, users can see the details on the financial coach’s Instagram page.

  • Fifth Page (Call to Action)

    After comprehending financial well-being's gravity and juxtaposing the two learning pathways (self-study vs. supported learning), and being further inspired by information from contents found in the resource and newsletter sections, users can make one of three informed decisions:

    • Subscribe to the newsletter.
    • Engage in a free introductory chat with the coach.
    • Secure a spot for the course.

    For subscribing and reserving a seat for the course, the website uses Formly. So when a user hits submit, the financial coach will receive an email. Similarly, for booking an introductory chat, the website integrates with the Calendly service to supplement our webpage’s lack of backend.

Accessibility

  • Strong Color Contrast & Alt Text

    Using the web accessibility evaluation tool, WAVE, I've corrected all the errors to ensure strong color contrast and appropriate alternative text for images.

  • Descriptive Link Text

    For the link descriptions, I have avoided using generic link texts such as “click here” or “read more.” Instead, I have opted for descriptive texts that clearly convey the purpose of each link. For instance, on the newsletter page, the button reads “Read more on Instagram.” Similarly, on the resource page, the button for recommended books states “View details on Amazon.”

  • Keyboard navigation

    To enhance accessibility for visually impaired users, I have employed the <button> tag combined with an event listener instead of the <a> tag. This methodology enables browsers to appropriately highlight the buttons. When users press "Enter", they are navigated to the designated link. I have instituted this approach on the Resources page, which contains links to recommended books, podcasts, and websites. In a similar vein, on the newsletter page, the "read more in Instagram" buttons—generated dynamically for each article via JavaScript—utilize the <button> tag as opposed to <a>.

Usability

  • Responsive Design

    For layout design, I've utilized Bootstrap's flex design with a column breakpoint for large screens. For instance, on the index page and the "Contact Us" page, an image and a paragraph are positioned next to each other, each occupying 50% of the space. However, in the mobile view, both the image and paragraph expand to take the full width. Similarly, on the "Course" and "Resource" pages, I've employed a card design. In the web view, three cards are displayed horizontally, but in the mobile view, each card expands to take up 100% of the width.

Learning

  • Firstly, I undertook this project for a real business owned by a friend who recently launched her venture. At the onset, the absence of a backend posed a challenge. However, I came across tools like "Form" that allow for input capture and automatically send her an email when a user submits information. I also integrated "Calendly" for booking introductory chats, allowing users to stay on the site rather than redirecting them to another platform. This integration was easier than I had anticipated.
  • Secondly, I deepened my understanding of Bootstrap. I consulted various YouTube tutorials and the official documentation. Specifically, features like carousels and accordions were straightforward to implement using Bootstrap.
  • Thirdly, I incorporated the use of handlebars. Given that my friend will need to update the website after I hand it over and she offers courses monthly, I streamlined the process for her. Instead of navigating a lengthy HTML document, she can simply update a JS file containing variables for upcoming group lesson dates, making it user-friendly.

Evaluataion I

One of the highlights of this project is that it's designed for a genuine business. This ensured that the feedback I received from my friend was both serious and of high quality. She posed challenging questions, such as the possibility of integrating Calendly directly instead of using a simple redirection button. Furthermore, her limited knowledge in coding motivated me to automate as much as possible.

With a proper backend, I envision a feature that directly integrates her Instagram posts, which she currently uses as a newsletter. Although this wasn't feasible for this course, I developed a simple JS script for her. With this script, she only needs to update a database-like file, which in turn populates a "newspaper archive" section formatted with images, descriptions, and dates. I recommended naming her images with dates so that the JS script can fetch and display the pertinent image for the day.

Evaluation II

  • Keyboard Navigation of Navbar and Footer

    While the WAVE tool did not highlight any errors, I encountered a navigation concern when using the ‘Tab’ key: the navbar items weren't correctly highlighted. This issue was initially prevalent with the <a> tags as well. To address it, I replaced these tags with <button> elements and incorporated event listeners to redirect to the respective links. While this workaround could be applied to the navbar, given the time constraints, I have decided to defer implementing this temporary solution. After submitting this assignment and before officially launching the website, I intend to delve deeper and address the root cause of this problem, which, at present, permits mouse click navigation but not keyboard input.

  • Lack of Clear Feedback on Form Submission

    Providing feedback is crucial when users complete actions like subscribing to a newsletter or reserving a seat for a course. This assurance lets users know that their input has been registered and processed. For this purpose, I crafted a tailored feedback page, `formspree_reply.html`, which should ideally display post-submission as it's located in the same folder. However, the default Formspree confirmation page appears instead of my customized page. The latter is designed to convey a more personalized acknowledgment, such as, "Thank you. Should you not hear back from us within two business days, please reach out to xxx."
